Unit 1.1

Basic Greetings

Formal vs. Informal Greetings

In English, we use different greetings depending on the situation:

  • Formal: Use with teachers, bosses, strangers, or in professional settings.
  • Informal: Use with friends, family, and people you know well.
English Portugus Usage
Hello Ol Formal/Informal
Hi Oi Informal
Good morning Bom dia Formal/Informal (until noon)
Good afternoon Boa tarde Formal/Informal (noon-6pm)
Good evening Boa noite Formal/Informal (after 6pm)
Goodbye Tchau / Adeus Formal/Informal
See you later At logo Informal
Tip:

In English, "Good night" is usually said when going to bed or leaving late at night, not as a greeting!

Examples

Formal

Good morning, Mr. Silva. How are you?

Bom dia, Sr. Silva. Como vai?

Informal

Hi Maria! How's it going?

Oi Maria! Como vai?

At the Office
Ana: Good morning, everyone!
Carlos: Good morning, Ana. How are you today?
Ana: I'm fine, thank you. And you?
Carlos: Very well, thanks!
Teacher Note:

Suggested time: 20 minutes

Activity: Have students practice the dialogue in pairs, then switch roles. Encourage them to substitute their own names.

Unit 1.2

Introducing Yourself

How to Introduce Yourself

When introducing yourself in English, use these patterns:

My name is [name].

I'm [name].

Nice to meet you.

Pleased to meet you. (more formal)

Note:

"I'm" is the contraction of "I am". Both are correct, but "I'm" is more common in spoken English.

Remember:

When someone says "Nice to meet you," respond with "Nice to meet you too!"

Example Conversation

First Day at a New Job
Pedro: Hello! My name is Pedro. I'm the new intern.
Sarah: Hi Pedro! I'm Sarah. Nice to meet you!
Pedro: Nice to meet you too, Sarah!
Sarah: Welcome to the team. Where are you from?
Pedro: I'm from Brazil. And you?
Sarah: I'm from Canada, but I live here now.
