Zero Class: Present Tense & Simple Past Review

Welcome to the Zero Class! Before we move forward, let's review what you already know. This chapter is a warm-up to make sure you feel confident with the Present Simple, Present Continuous, and Simple Past.

Unit 4.1

Present Tense Review

📐 Present Simple: Formation

We use the Present Simple to talk about habits, routines, facts, and things that are always or generally true.

Form Structure Example
Affirmative Subject + verb (+s/es for he/she/it) She drinks coffee every morning.
Negative Subject + do/does + not + base verb They don't watch TV at night.
Interrogative Do/Does + subject + base verb? Does he walk to work?

Subject-verb agreement: Remember to add -s or -es for he/she/it:

  • Most verbs: add -s (cook → cooks, run → runs)
  • Verbs ending in -ch, -sh, -ss, -x, -o: add -es (teach → teaches, miss → misses)
  • Verbs ending in consonant + y: change y to -ies (carry → carries, try → tries)
  • Irregular: have → has, be → is
📐 Present Continuous: Formation and Usage

We use the Present Continuous (also called Present Progressive) to talk about actions happening right now or around this time.

Form Structure Example
Affirmative Subject + am/is/are + verb-ing She is reading a book right now.
Negative Subject + am/is/are + not + verb-ing I am not watching TV.
Interrogative Am/Is/Are + subject + verb-ing? Are they studying?

When to use each tense:

  • Present Simple: habits, routines, general facts (I walk to school every day.)
  • Present Continuous: actions happening now, temporary situations (I am walking to school right now.)

Present Simple vs. Present Continuous

Habit vs. Now

She cooks dinner every evening. (habit)

She is cooking dinner right now. (happening now)

Ela cozinha o jantar toda noite. / Ela está cozinhando o jantar agora.

Routine vs. Temporary

I walk to school every day. (routine)

Today I am walking with my friend. (temporary)

Eu caminho até a escola todo dia. / Hoje eu estou caminhando com meu amigo.

Fact vs. Current action

He teaches math at our school. (general fact)

Right now, he is teaching a new lesson. (current action)

Ele ensina matemática na nossa escola. / Agora ele está ensinando uma lição nova.

Always vs. At the moment

They listen to music after school. (always)

They are listening to a new song at the moment. (at the moment)

Eles ouvem música depois da escola. / Eles estão ouvindo uma música nova neste momento.

Negative contrast

I don't run on rainy days. (habit)

I am not running today because it's raining. (right now)

Eu não corro em dias chuvosos. / Eu não estou correndo hoje porque está chovendo.

Question contrast

Do you read before bed? (habit)

Are you reading that book? (right now)

Você lê antes de dormir? / Você está lendo aquele livro?

💡
Tip: Common Mistakes
  • "I am go" → "I am going" (always use verb + -ing with am/is/are)
  • "She cook every day" → "She cooks every day" (don't forget -s for he/she/it)
  • "He is works now" → "He is working now" (don't mix the two tenses)
  • "I am liking chocolate" → "I like chocolate" (some verbs are not normally used in continuous: like, love, want, know, need)
Teacher Note:

Suggested time: 30 minutes

Warm-up activity: Write two columns on the board: "Every day" and "Right now." Ask students to give examples for each. This activates the contrast between Present Simple and Present Continuous.

Key point: Many A1 students confuse the two tenses. Emphasize the time markers: "every day, always, usually" (Simple) vs. "right now, at the moment, today" (Continuous).

Unit 4.2

Simple Past Review

📐 Simple Past: Regular Verbs (-ed Rules)

We use the Simple Past to talk about actions that started and finished in the past.

How to form the past of regular verbs:

Rule Example Past Form
Most verbs: add -ed walk, clean, help walked, cleaned, helped
Verbs ending in -e: add -d dance, live, arrive danced, lived, arrived
Consonant + y: change y to -ied carry, study, try carried, studied, tried
Short CVC verbs: double last letter + -ed stop, plan, chat stopped, planned, chatted

Pronunciation of -ed:

  • /t/ after voiceless sounds: walked, helped, cooked
  • /d/ after voiced sounds: cleaned, listened, opened
  • /ɪd/ after -t or -d: waited, needed, wanted
📐 Irregular Verbs: Top 20 Past Forms

These common verbs do not follow the -ed pattern. You need to memorize them!

Negative and question forms use did + base verb (not the past form):

  • She went home. → She didn't go home. → Did she go home?

Dialogue: What Did You Do Last Weekend?

Monday Morning in the Classroom
Rafaela: Good morning, Tiago! How was your weekend?
Tiago: It was really fun! On Saturday, I went to my grandmother's house.
Rafaela: That's nice! What did you do there?
Tiago: She made a big lunch. We ate rice, beans, and chicken. It was delicious!
Rafaela: Sounds wonderful! Did you do anything on Sunday?
Tiago: Yes! I played soccer with my cousins in the park. We played for two hours. What about you?
Rafaela: On Saturday, I stayed home and studied for the English test. I didn't go out.
Tiago: Oh no! Was it boring?
Rafaela: A little bit. But on Sunday, I went to the shopping center with my mom. We bought new clothes and ate pizza.
Tiago: That sounds great! Did you buy anything cool?
Rafaela: Yes, I got a new backpack for school. I really like it!
Tiago: Cool! This weekend was good for both of us.

Remember: Time Expressions for the Past

These words and phrases tell us the action happened in the past:

  • yesterday (ontem)
  • last week / last month / last year (semana passada / mês passado / ano passado)
  • ... ago (two days ago, three years ago) (... atrás)
  • in 2020 / in January (em 2020 / em janeiro)
  • on Monday / on Saturday (na segunda / no sábado)
  • this morning (esta manhã — when the morning is over)
Teacher Note:

Suggested time: 30 minutes

Pronunciation activity: Read the irregular verbs table aloud with the class. Have students repeat each pair (base → past). Then play a quick game: say the base form, students say the past.

Dialogue activity: Have students read the dialogue in pairs. Then ask each student to tell their partner about their own real weekend using at least 4 past tense verbs.

Unit 4.3

Reading: My Routine and My Unusual Day

Beatriz's Day Read the text carefully, then answer the questions below.

Beatriz is fifteen years old and lives in Recife. Every morning, she wakes up at six thirty and takes a shower. She eats bread and fruit for breakfast and drinks a glass of juice. She walks to school with her neighbor, Clara. Classes start at seven fifteen. Beatriz likes math and art, but she doesn't like history very much. After school, she usually goes home, does her homework, and helps her mother in the kitchen. In the evening, she watches TV or reads a book before bed.

Yesterday was different. Beatriz woke up late because she forgot to set her alarm. She didn't have time for breakfast, so she only drank some water. She ran to school alone because Clara was sick and stayed home. Beatriz arrived five minutes late and the teacher said, "Please be on time tomorrow." In the afternoon, she didn't go home after school. She went to the library with her friend Mateus and they studied for the science test together. They stayed at the library for two hours. When Beatriz got home, her mother had made a special dinner: lasagna! Beatriz was very happy. She ate two servings and then went to bed early because she was tired from the long day.

Original text for Incluir English Workbook
Teacher Note:

Pre-reading activity: Ask students: "What is different when you wake up late? What changes in your routine?" This activates the contrast between normal routine (present) and an unusual day (past).

Reading strategy: First reading: understand the general idea. Second reading: underline verbs in the first paragraph (present tense) and circle verbs in the second paragraph (past tense).
