📐 How to Talk About Feelings / Como Falar Sobre Sentimentos

In English, we use the verb to be (am / is / are) to talk about feelings. / Em ingles, usamos o verbo "to be" (am / is / are) para falar de sentimentos.

English Portugues
I am happy. Eu estou feliz.
You are tired. Voce esta cansado(a).
He is angry. Ele esta com raiva.
She is sad. Ela esta triste.
We are excited. Nos estamos animados.
They are scared. Eles estao com medo.

Question / Pergunta:

  • How are you? = Como voce esta?
  • I am fine, thank you. = Eu estou bem, obrigado(a).
  • How is she? = Como ela esta?
  • She is happy. = Ela esta feliz.

Examples / Exemplos

Affirmative / Afirmativa

I am happy today.

Eu estou feliz hoje.

Affirmative / Afirmativa

Carlos is tired after school.

Carlos esta cansado depois da escola.

Affirmative / Afirmativa

The children are hungry.

As criancas estao com fome.

Question / Pergunta

How are you? - I am fine, thank you.

Como voce esta? - Eu estou bem, obrigado(a).

Negative / Negativa

She is not angry. She is sad.

Ela nao esta com raiva. Ela esta triste.

📌
Remember / Lembre-se:

In Portuguese we say "ter fome", "ter sede", "ter medo", "ter sono". But in English we say "be hungry", "be thirsty", "be scared", "be sleepy".

Em portugues dizemos "ter fome", "ter sede", "ter medo", "ter sono". Mas em ingles usamos o verbo "be" (estar).

  • I am hungry. (NOT: I have hungry.) / Eu estou com fome. (NAO: Eu tenho fome.)
  • She is thirsty. (NOT: She has thirsty.) / Ela esta com sede.
  • He is scared. (NOT: He has scared.) / Ele esta com medo.
  • I am sleepy. (NOT: I have sleepy.) / Eu estou com sono.
Teacher Note:

Suggested time: 25 minutes

Warm-up: Draw simple emoji faces on the board (happy, sad, angry, tired, scared). Point to each face and say the word slowly. Have students repeat. Then ask: "How are you today?" and let each student answer with a feeling word.

L1 interference: Portuguese speakers will naturally say "I have hungry" or "I have scared" because in Portuguese we use "ter" (to have) for these expressions. Emphasize that English uses "be" (am/is/are) for ALL feelings. Write on the board: "ter fome = BE hungry" and drill several examples.

Activity: Play "Feelings Charades" (Mimica de Sentimentos). One student acts out a feeling. The class guesses: "You are tired!" / "She is happy!"

Dialogue / Dialogo

Rafaela is showing family photos to her new classmate, Joao. / Rafaela esta mostrando fotos da familia para seu novo colega, Joao.

At School - Break Time / Na Escola - Hora do Intervalo
Joao: Rafaela, is that your family? (Rafaela, essa e sua familia?)
Rafaela: Yes! Look! This is my mother. Her name is Claudia. (Sim! Olha! Esta e minha mae. O nome dela e Claudia.)
Joao: She is very pretty! And who is this? (Ela e muito bonita! E quem e esse?)
Rafaela: This is my father. His name is Marcos. He is a cook. (Este e meu pai. O nome dele e Marcos. Ele e cozinheiro.)
Joao: Cool! Do you have brothers or sisters? (Legal! Voce tem irmaos ou irmas?)
Rafaela: I have one brother. His name is Lucas. He is 8 years old. And this is my baby sister. Her name is Sofia. (Eu tenho um irmao. O nome dele e Lucas. Ele tem 8 anos. E esta e minha irma bebe. O nome dela e Sofia.)
Joao: She is cute! And who are they? (Ela e fofa! E quem sao eles?)
Rafaela: They are my grandparents. My grandmother is Dona Maria and my grandfather is Seu Antonio. They live in Belo Horizonte. (Eles sao meus avos. Minha avo e Dona Maria e meu avo e Seu Antonio. Eles moram em Belo Horizonte.)
Joao: Nice! Your family is big! (Legal! Sua familia e grande!)
Rafaela: Yes, it is! And I love my family! (Sim! E eu amo minha familia!)
Teacher Note:

Suggested time: 25 minutes

Warm-up: Draw a simple family tree on the board with stick figures. Label each figure: mother, father, sister, brother, grandmother, grandfather. Have students repeat each word after you.

Dialogue activity: Have students read the dialogue in pairs. Then swap roles. After reading, ask students to bring a family photo (or draw one) and practice the same dialogue structure with their own family names.

False friend alert: Emphasize "parents" vs. "parentes" (relatives). This is one of the most common false friends for Brazilian learners.

Unit 7.3

Talking About Your Family / Falando Sobre Sua Familia

📐 Possessive Adjectives / Adjetivos Possessivos

Possessive adjectives show who something belongs to. / Adjetivos possessivos mostram a quem algo pertence.

English Portugues Example / Exemplo
my meu / minha My name is Pedro. / Meu nome e Pedro.
your seu / sua / teu / tua Your mother is nice. / Sua mae e legal.
his dele His father is a doctor. / O pai dele e medico.
her dela Her brother is tall. / O irmao dela e alto.

Important / Importante:

  • his = always for a boy or man / sempre para menino ou homem
  • her = always for a girl or woman / sempre para menina ou mulher

Em ingles, "his" e "her" nao mudam de acordo com a coisa possuida. "His" e sempre para ELE e "her" e sempre para ELA.

  • His mother. His father. His book. (all = dele)
  • Her mother. Her father. Her book. (all = dela)

Vocabulary / Vocabulario

English Portugues Example Sentence / Frase Exemplo
my meu / minha My family is small. / Minha familia e pequena.
your seu / sua What is your name? / Qual e o seu nome?
his dele His mother is a nurse. / A mae dele e enfermeira.
her dela Her sister is 5 years old. / A irma dela tem 5 anos.

Examples / Exemplos

my

My name is Fernanda. My mother is a teacher.

Meu nome e Fernanda. Minha mae e professora.

your

What is your father's name? Is your sister in this school?

Qual e o nome do seu pai? Sua irma estuda nesta escola?

his

This is Carlos. His brother is tall. His mother is a doctor.

Este e Carlos. O irmao dele e alto. A mae dele e medica.

her

This is Mariana. Her father is funny. Her grandmother lives in Bahia.

Esta e Mariana. O pai dela e engracado. A avo dela mora na Bahia.

💡
Tip / Dica:

In Portuguese, "seu/sua" changes depending on the THING being talked about: "seu livro" (masc.) / "sua casa" (fem.). But in English, "his" and "her" change depending on the PERSON who owns it, not the thing!

Em portugues, "seu/sua" muda de acordo com a COISA: "seu livro" / "sua casa". Mas em ingles, "his" e "her" mudam de acordo com a PESSOA que possui, nao a coisa!

  • Carlos... his book, his house, his mother (everything is "his" because Carlos is a boy / tudo e "his" porque Carlos e menino)
  • Ana... her book, her house, her father (everything is "her" because Ana is a girl / tudo e "her" porque Ana e menina)
Teacher Note:

Suggested time: 20 minutes

Warm-up: Point to yourself and say "my book." Point to a student and say "your book." Point to a boy and say "his book." Point to a girl and say "her book." Repeat with different objects until students understand the pattern.

L1 interference: Brazilian Portuguese speakers often confuse "his" and "her" because in Portuguese the possessive agrees with the noun gender, not the possessor's gender. Practice with several examples: "Ana loves HER brother. Carlos loves HIS brother."

Activity: Have students sit in pairs. Student A says a sentence about a classmate using "his" or "her": "Her name is Julia." Student B points to the correct person.

Reading / Leitura

Read the text carefully. Then do the exercises below. / Leia o texto com atencao. Depois faca os exercicios abaixo.

Maria's Family / A Familia da Maria Read the text carefully, then answer the questions below. / Leia o texto com atencao, depois responda as perguntas abaixo.

Hi! My name is Maria. I am 13 years old. I live in Fortaleza with my family.

(Oi! Meu nome e Maria. Eu tenho 13 anos. Eu moro em Fortaleza com a minha familia.)

My family is not very big. I live with my mother, my father, and my brother. My mother's name is Claudia. She is a nurse. She works at a hospital. My father's name is Roberto. He is a taxi driver. He is very funny.

(Minha familia nao e muito grande. Eu moro com minha mae, meu pai e meu irmao. O nome da minha mae e Claudia. Ela e enfermeira. Ela trabalha em um hospital. O nome do meu pai e Roberto. Ele e taxista. Ele e muito engracado.)

My brother's name is Felipe. He is 8 years old. He is very excited today because it is his birthday! My mother is happy because all the family is together. My grandmother is here too. Her name is Dona Rosa. She is 65 years old. She lives in Recife, but today she is in Fortaleza for Felipe's birthday.

(O nome do meu irmao e Felipe. Ele tem 8 anos. Ele esta muito animado hoje porque e o aniversario dele! Minha mae esta feliz porque toda a familia esta junta. Minha avo tambem esta aqui. O nome dela e Dona Rosa. Ela tem 65 anos. Ela mora em Recife, mas hoje ela esta em Fortaleza para o aniversario do Felipe.)

I am happy today too. But my father is a little tired because he is working a lot this week. And my grandmother is worried about the rain, because she wants to go back to Recife tomorrow.

(Eu estou feliz hoje tambem. Mas meu pai esta um pouco cansado porque ele esta trabalhando muito esta semana. E minha avo esta preocupada com a chuva, porque ela quer voltar para Recife amanha.)

Original text for Incluir English Workbook
Teacher Note:

Suggested time: 30 minutes (10 for reading, 20 for exercises)

Pre-reading activity: Ask students: "How many people are in your family? Who are they?" Allow them to answer in Portuguese if needed, then help them say it in English.

Reading strategy: Have students underline all family words in one color and circle all feeling words in another color while reading. Then have them underline all possessive adjectives (my, his, her) in a third color.

Differentiation: For struggling students, read the text aloud first while they follow along. For stronger students, have them try reading the English text before looking at the Portuguese translations.
