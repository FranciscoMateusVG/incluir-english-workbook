Unit 2.1

The English Alphabet (O Alfabeto em Inglês)

The English alphabet has 26 letters. It is the same alphabet we use in Portuguese, but the pronunciation is very different!

O alfabeto inglês tem 26 letras. É o mesmo alfabeto que usamos em português, mas a pronúncia é muito diferente!

Letters That Sound Very Different from Portuguese

Letras que soam muito diferentes do português

Letter (Letra) English Sound (Som em Inglês) Portuguese Sound (Som em Português) Watch Out! (Atenção!)
A "êi" /eɪ/ "á" In English, A sounds like "êi", not "á"!
E "i" /iː/ "é" In English, E sounds like "i", not "é"!
G "dji" /dʒiː/ "gê" G starts with a "dj" sound!
H "êitch" /eɪtʃ/ "agá" Very different! "Êitch", not "agá"!
I "ái" /aɪ/ "i" In English, I sounds like "ái", not "i"!
J "djêi" /dʒeɪ/ "jota" J sounds like "djêi", not "jota"!
R "ár" /ɑːr/ "érre" Short and soft: "ár"!
W "dâbliu" /ˈdʌbəl.juː/ "dáblio" Say "dâbliu", not "dáblio"!
Y "uái" /waɪ/ "ípsilon" Y sounds like "uái", not "ípsilon"!
💡
Tip - How to spell your name in English:

Question: "How do you spell your name?" (Como se soletra seu nome?)

Answer: "My name is Ana. A-N-A." (Meu nome é Ana. A-N-A.)

Example: "My name is Lucas. L-U-C-A-S." (Meu nome é Lucas. L-U-C-A-S.)

In English, we say each letter one by one. We do not say syllables.
Em inglês, dizemos cada letra uma por uma. Não dizemos sílabas.

Teacher Note:

Suggested time: 20 minutes

Pronunciation activity: Say each letter out loud as a class. Then have each student spell their own name. You can also play "Alphabet Bingo" - call out letters and students mark them on a card.

L1 note: Brazilian students commonly struggle with H (/eɪtʃ/ vs. "agá"), J (/dʒeɪ/ vs. "jota"), and the vowel letters (A, E, I). Drill these specifically.

Useful Sentences with Numbers (Frases Úteis com Números)

Question

How old are you?

Quantos anos você tem?

Answer

I am fourteen years old.

Eu tenho quatorze anos.

Question

What is your phone number?

Qual é o seu número de telefone?

Answer

My phone number is nine-eight-seven-six-five.

Meu número de telefone é nove-oito-sete-seis-cinco.

Question

How many students are in your class?

Quantos alunos há na sua turma?

Answer

There are twenty-five students.

Há vinte e cinco alunos.

Teacher Note:

Suggested time: 25 minutes

Activity: Have students count around the room (1, 2, 3...). Then practice in pairs: "How old are you?" / "I am ___ years old." You can also do a "Number Dictation" - say a number and students write it.

Common errors: Students often confuse "thirteen/thirty", "fourteen/forty", "fifteen/fifty" etc. Practice the stress difference: thirTEEN vs. THIRty.

English (Inglês) Português Example (Exemplo)

Example 4

Example 5

🧠
Remember!

In English, colors do NOT change for plural or gender. The color word is always the same!
Em inglês, as cores NÃO mudam no plural ou no gênero. A palavra da cor é sempre a mesma!

  • "a red car" = "um carro vermelho" (singular)
  • "two red cars" = "dois carros vermelhos" (plural - red stays the same!)
  • "a red apple" = "uma maçã vermelha" (feminine - red stays the same!)

WRONG: "two reds cars" / RIGHT: "two red cars"

Teacher Note:

Suggested time: 15 minutes

Activity: Bring colored objects (pencils, papers, etc.) to class. Hold up each object and ask "What color is this?" Students answer: "It is ___." You can also ask students to find objects of a given color in the classroom.

L1 note: In Portuguese, colors agree in gender and number ("carros vermelhos"). Emphasize that English colors NEVER change form.

Animals (Animais)

Now let's learn the names of common animals in English! (Agora vamos aprender os nomes de animais comuns em inglês!)

Animal Vocabulary (Vocabulário de Animais)

English (Inglês) Português Example (Exemplo)
dog cachorro / cão I have a dog. (Eu tenho um cachorro.)
cat gato(a) The cat is black. (O gato é preto.)
bird pássaro / ave The bird is in the tree. (O pássaro está na árvore.)

Talking About Animals (Falando Sobre Animais)

Saying you have a pet

I have a dog.

Eu tenho um cachorro.

Saying you have a pet

I have two cats.

Eu tenho dois gatos.

Asking about animals

Do you like dogs?

Você gosta de cachorros?

Answering

Yes, I like dogs. / No, I don't like snakes.

Sim, eu gosto de cachorros. / Não, eu não gosto de cobras.

Describing

My dog is big and brown.

Meu cachorro é grande e marrom.

💡
Tip - Similar Words!

Some animal names are similar in Portuguese and English. This makes them easier to remember!
Alguns nomes de animais são parecidos em português e inglês. Isso os torna mais fáceis de lembrar!

  • elephant = elefante
  • lion = leão
  • monkey sounds a bit like "macaco" (but it is different!)

But watch out! Some are very different:
Mas cuidado! Alguns são muito diferentes:

  • dog (not "dogue") = cachorro
  • butterfly = borboleta (very different!)
  • rabbit = coelho (very different!)
Teacher Note:

Suggested time: 20 minutes

Activity: Play "Animal Charades" - one student acts like an animal, others guess in English. You can also play "Animal Bingo" with picture cards. Ask students: "Do you have a pet?" and practice the response "I have a ___" / "I don't have a pet."

Extension: Combine with colors! "What color is the elephant?" → "The elephant is gray."

My favorite animal is the elephant. Elephants are big and gray. I think they are beautiful!
