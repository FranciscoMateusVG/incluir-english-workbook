Chapter 5

WH Questions, Hobbies and Professions, Countries and Nationalities

Neste capitulo, voce vai aprender a fazer perguntas em ingles usando palavras como What, Where e Who. Tambem vai aprender vocabulario sobre hobbies (passatempos), professions (profissoes) e countries (paises).

In this chapter you will learn to ask questions in English using words like What, Where, and Who. You will also learn vocabulary about hobbies, professions, and countries.

Unit 5.1

WH Questions - Perguntas WH

📐 WH Words - Palavras para Perguntas

Em ingles, usamos palavras especiais para fazer perguntas. Elas comecam com WH (por isso chamamos de "WH questions").

In English, we use special words to ask questions. They start with WH (that is why we call them "WH questions").

WH Word Portugues We use it for... (Usamos para...)
What? O que? / Qual? things (coisas)
Where? Onde? places (lugares)
When? Quando? time (tempo)
Who? Quem? people (pessoas)
Why? Por que? reasons (motivos)
How? Como? manner (maneira)
📐 How to Make a WH Question - Como Fazer uma Pergunta WH

A estrutura basica e muito simples:

The basic structure is very simple:

WH word + is / are + ...?

WH Word + is / are + rest of the question
What is your name?
Where are you from?
Who is your teacher?
When is the class?
Why are you happy?
How are you?

Examples - Exemplos

What

What is your name? — My name is Ana.

Qual e o seu nome? — Meu nome e Ana.

Where

Where are you from? — I am from Brazil.

De onde voce e? — Eu sou do Brasil.

When

When is your birthday? — My birthday is in March.

Quando e o seu aniversario? — Meu aniversario e em marco.

Who

Who is your best friend? — My best friend is Pedro.

Quem e o seu melhor amigo? — Meu melhor amigo e o Pedro.

Why

Why are you happy? — Because today is my birthday!

Por que voce esta feliz? — Porque hoje e meu aniversario!

How

How are you? — I am fine, thank you.

Como voce esta? — Eu estou bem, obrigado(a).

💡
Tip - Dica:

WH questions have a falling intonation (a voz desce no final). Compare:

  • Yes/No question (voz sobe): "Are you from Brazil?" ↑
  • WH question (voz desce): "Where are you from?" ↓

Pratique dizendo as perguntas em voz alta! (Practise saying the questions out loud!)

Teacher Note:

Suggested time: 20 minutes

Warm-up: Write each WH word on a separate card. Hold up a card and ask students to say the Portuguese translation. Then give a simple example question for each one.

L1 support: At the B0 level, students need extensive Portuguese scaffolding. Allow them to first understand the meaning in Portuguese before producing English questions. Use gestures and visual cues to reinforce meaning.

Pronunciation note: Practise the "wh" sound carefully. Many students will want to say it as a Portuguese "v" sound.

📐 Talking About Hobbies and Professions - Falando Sobre Passatempos e Profissoes

Para falar sobre passatempos (hobbies):

English Portugues
I like to play soccer. Eu gosto de jogar futebol.
She likes to read books. Ela gosta de ler livros.
What do you like to do? O que voce gosta de fazer?

Para falar sobre profissoes (professions):

English Portugues
I am a student. Eu sou estudante.
He is a doctor. Ele e medico.
What is your profession? Qual e a sua profissao?

Examples - Exemplos

Hobby

I like to play soccer and watch TV.

Eu gosto de jogar futebol e assistir TV.

Hobby

She likes to draw and listen to music.

Ela gosta de desenhar e ouvir musica.

Profession

My mother is a nurse. She works at the hospital.

Minha mae e enfermeira. Ela trabalha no hospital.

Question

What do you like to do? — I like to swim and cook.

O que voce gosta de fazer? — Eu gosto de nadar e cozinhar.

Question

What is your profession? — I am a teacher.

Qual e a sua profissao? — Eu sou professor(a).

Dialogue - Dialogo

Camila and Rafael talk about their hobbies at school. (Camila e Rafael conversam sobre seus passatempos na escola.)
Camila: Hi, Rafael! What do you like to do after school?
(Oi, Rafael! O que voce gosta de fazer depois da escola?)
Rafael: I like to play soccer and play video games. And you?
(Eu gosto de jogar futebol e jogar video game. E voce?)
Camila: I like to draw and listen to music. I also like to dance!
(Eu gosto de desenhar e ouvir musica. Eu tambem gosto de dancar!)
Rafael: Nice! What is your mother's profession?
(Legal! Qual e a profissao da sua mae?)
Camila: She is a nurse. And your father?
(Ela e enfermeira. E o seu pai?)
Rafael: He is an engineer. He likes to cook too!
(Ele e engenheiro. Ele gosta de cozinhar tambem!)
Teacher Note:

Suggested time: 25 minutes

Activity: After presenting the vocabulary tables, have students practise the dialogue in pairs. Then ask them to create a similar dialogue using their own hobbies and their family members' professions.

Note on articles: At B0 level, do not overemphasize the a/an distinction with professions. Simply model correct usage (a teacher, an engineer) and correct gently when needed.

Cognates: Point out cognates (palavras parecidas) like "doctor / doutor", "engineer / engenheiro", "dentist / dentista" to build confidence.

📐 Talking About Countries and Nationalities - Falando Sobre Paises e Nacionalidades

Existem duas formas de dizer de onde voce e:

There are two ways to say where you are from:

Structure (Estrutura) Example (Exemplo) Portugues
I am from + country I am from Brazil. Eu sou do Brasil.
I am + nationality I am Brazilian. Eu sou brasileiro(a).
She is from + country She is from Japan. Ela e do Japao.
She is + nationality She is Japanese. Ela e japonesa.

To ask (Para perguntar):

  • Where are you from? (De onde voce e?)
  • What is your nationality? (Qual e a sua nacionalidade?)

Examples - Exemplos

Country

I am from Brazil. I am Brazilian.

Eu sou do Brasil. Eu sou brasileiro(a).

Country

She is from France. She is French.

Ela e da Franca. Ela e francesa.

Country

He is from Mexico. He is Mexican.

Ele e do Mexico. Ele e mexicano.

Question

Where are you from? — I am from Argentina. I am Argentine.

De onde voce e? — Eu sou da Argentina. Eu sou argentino(a).

Question

Where is your friend from? — He is from Italy. He is Italian.

De onde e o seu amigo? — Ele e da Italia. Ele e italiano.

🧠
Remember - Lembre-se:

Nationalities are always capitalized in English! (Nacionalidades sempre comecam com letra maiuscula em ingles!)

  • Correct: She is Brazilian. He is Japanese.
  • Incorrect: She is brazilian. He is japanese.

Em portugues, nacionalidades usam letra minuscula ("brasileiro"). Em ingles, sempre letra maiuscula ("Brazilian").

Teacher Note:

Suggested time: 20 minutes

Activity: Bring a world map or project one on the board. Point to each country and practise the pronunciation of both the country name and nationality. Have students repeat after you. Then ask individual students: "Where are you from?" and model the full answer: "I am from Brazil. I am Brazilian."

Cultural note: This is a great opportunity to discuss the diversity of Brazil and how people from many different countries live in Brazilian cities. Ask students if they know anyone from another country.

Spelling patterns: Help students notice patterns: -an (American, Mexican), -ese (Japanese, Chinese, Portuguese), -ish (English), -ch (French).

Unit 5.4

Reading & Writing - Leitura & Escrita

My New Friend Read the text carefully, then answer the questions below. (Leia o texto com atencao e depois responda as perguntas.)

My name is Fernanda. I am 13 years old. I am from Brazil. I am Brazilian. I am a student at Escola Incluir in Belo Horizonte.

Today there is a new student in my class. His name is Kenji. He is from Japan. He is Japanese. He is 14 years old. He is very nice.

"Hi, Kenji! Where are you from?" I ask.

"I am from Tokyo, Japan," Kenji answers.

"What do you like to do?" I ask.

"I like to draw and play video games. And you?" Kenji says.

"I like to dance and listen to music!" I say. "Who is your favourite singer?"

"I like Ado. She is Japanese. She is a singer," Kenji answers.

"Nice! What is your father's profession?" I ask.

"My father is an engineer. And your mother?" Kenji asks.

"My mother is a teacher. She is an English teacher!" I say.

"That is great! English is my favourite subject," Kenji says.

Now Kenji is my friend. He is from Japan and I am from Brazil, but we like the same things!

Original text for Incluir English Workbook
💡
Vocabulary Help - Ajuda de Vocabulario:
  • new = novo(a)
  • today = hoje
  • nice = legal / gentil
  • favourite = favorito(a)
  • singer = cantor(a)
  • subject = materia (escolar)
  • same = mesmo(a)
  • but = mas
Teacher Note:

Pre-reading activity: Ask students: "Do you have a friend from another country? What country?" Write some countries on the board. This activates prior knowledge and introduces the text topic.

Reading strategy: For B0 students, read the text aloud first while students follow along. Then have students read it silently. Encourage them to underline words they recognize. Point out that many words in the text were covered in this chapter.

Comprehension check: After reading, ask simple oral questions before moving to the written exercises: "What is the girl's name?" "Where is Kenji from?"
