📐 Talking About Your Body / Falando Sobre Seu Corpo

Use these sentences to talk about body parts. / Use estas frases para falar sobre partes do corpo.

English Portugues When to use / Quando usar
This is my ___. Este(a) e o/a meu/minha ___. One body part (singular) / Uma parte do corpo (singular)
These are my ___. Estes(as) sao os/as meus/minhas ___. Two or more body parts (plural) / Duas ou mais partes (plural)
I have two ___. Eu tenho dois/duas ___. Saying how many / Dizendo quantos

Examples / Exemplos

Singular (one) / Singular (um)

This is my head.

Esta e a minha cabeca.

Singular (one) / Singular (um)

This is my nose.

Este e o meu nariz.

Plural (two or more) / Plural (dois ou mais)

These are my hands.

Estas sao as minhas maos.

Plural (two or more) / Plural (dois ou mais)

I have two eyes.

Eu tenho dois olhos.

Irregular plural / Plural irregular

I have two feet.

Eu tenho dois pes.

💡
Tip / Dica:

Some body parts have irregular plurals. They do NOT add "-s". / Algumas partes do corpo tem plurais irregulares. Eles NAO adicionam "-s".

  • tooth (1 dente) → teeth (dentes) -- NOT "tooths"
  • foot (1 pe) → feet (pes) -- NOT "foots"

Most other body parts are regular: eye → eyes, arm → arms, leg → legs. / A maioria das outras partes do corpo sao regulares: eye → eyes, arm → arms, leg → legs.

Teacher Note:

Suggested time: 25 minutes

TPR Activity (Total Physical Response): Say body part names and have students touch the correct body part. Start slowly: "Touch your nose!", "Touch your head!", "Touch your ears!" Increase the speed as students gain confidence. This is a very effective technique for absolute beginners because it connects words to physical actions.

Game idea: Play "Simon Says" using body parts. "Simon says touch your knee!" (students touch their knee). "Touch your shoulder!" (students should NOT move because you didn't say "Simon says"). This is fun and reinforces vocabulary through movement.

L1 support: At B0 level, always say the Portuguese translation after introducing each new word. Point to the body part on yourself or on the diagram as you say each word.

📐 Talking About Health Problems / Falando Sobre Problemas de Saude

There are two main ways to talk about health problems in English. / Ha duas formas principais de falar sobre problemas de saude em ingles.

Structure / Estrutura Example / Exemplo Portugues
I have a ___. I have a headache. Eu tenho dor de cabeca.
I have a ___. I have a cold. Eu estou resfriado(a).
I have a ___. I have a fever. Eu estou com febre.
I have a ___. I have a cough. Eu estou com tosse.
My ___ hurts. My head hurts. Minha cabeca doi.
My ___ hurts. My stomach hurts. Meu estomago doi.
My ___ hurts. My back hurts. Minhas costas doem.
I am sick. I am sick. Eu estou doente.
I need to see a doctor. I need to see a doctor. Eu preciso ir ao medico.

Examples / Exemplos

I have a ___ / Eu tenho ___

I have a headache. I can't go to school.

Eu tenho dor de cabeca. Eu nao posso ir a escola.

I have a ___ / Eu tenho ___

Ana has a cold. She is sneezing.

Ana esta resfriada. Ela esta espirrando.

My ___ hurts. / Meu/Minha ___ doi.

My stomach hurts. I ate too much.

Meu estomago doi. Eu comi demais.

I am sick. / Eu estou doente.

I am sick. I need to rest.

Eu estou doente. Eu preciso descansar.

I need to see a doctor. / Eu preciso ir ao medico.

My tooth hurts a lot. I need to see a doctor.

Meu dente doi muito. Eu preciso ir ao medico.

📌
Remember / Lembre-se:

"I have a headache." (Eu tenho dor de cabeca.) -- In English we use "have" for health problems, just like in Portuguese! / Em ingles usamos "have" (ter) para problemas de saude, igual ao portugues!

"My head hurts." (Minha cabeca doi.) -- This is also correct! Both forms mean the same thing. / Esta forma tambem esta correta! As duas formas significam a mesma coisa.

Important / Importante: We say "I have a headache" (with "a"). / Dizemos "I have a headache" (com "a").

Teacher Note:

Suggested time: 20 minutes

Activity: Act out health problems! Make a gesture for each problem (hold your head for headache, hold your stomach for stomachache, cough for cough, etc.). Have students guess the health problem in English.

Pair work: Student A acts sick. Student B asks: "What's wrong?" Student A answers: "I have a ___." or "My ___ hurts." Then switch roles.

L1 support: Students at B0 level will naturally want to say things in Portuguese first. That's fine -- help them translate into English. The key structures to drill are "I have a ___" and "My ___ hurts."

Unit 8.3

At the Doctor's Office / No Consultorio Medico

Dialogue / Dialogo: A Visit to the Doctor / Uma Visita ao Medico

At the doctor's office / No consultorio medico

Doctor: Good morning! What's your name?

Medico: Bom dia! Qual e o seu nome?

Pedro: Good morning, doctor. My name is Pedro.

Pedro: Bom dia, doutor. Meu nome e Pedro.

Doctor: What's wrong, Pedro?

Medico: O que esta errado, Pedro? / Qual e o problema?

Pedro: I feel sick. My head hurts and I have a fever.

Pedro: Eu me sinto mal. Minha cabeca doi e eu estou com febre.

Doctor: Let me check. Yes, you have a fever. Take this medicine.

Medico: Deixe-me verificar. Sim, voce esta com febre. Tome este remedio.

Pedro: Thank you, doctor.

Pedro: Obrigado, doutor.

Doctor: Get some rest. Drink water. Come back tomorrow.

Medico: Descanse um pouco. Beba agua. Volte amanha.

Pedro: OK. Goodbye, doctor.

Pedro: OK. Tchau, doutor.

Doctor: Goodbye, Pedro. Get well soon!

Medico: Tchau, Pedro. Melhoras!

📐 Useful Phrases at the Doctor / Frases Uteis no Medico

Learn these important phrases for a doctor's visit. / Aprenda estas frases importantes para uma visita ao medico.

English Portugues Who says it / Quem fala
What's wrong? O que esta errado? / Qual e o problema? Doctor / Medico
What's the problem? Qual e o problema? Doctor / Medico
I feel sick. Eu me sinto mal. Patient / Paciente
My ___ hurts. Meu/Minha ___ doi. Patient / Paciente
I have a ___. Eu tenho ___. Patient / Paciente
Take this medicine. Tome este remedio. Doctor / Medico
Get some rest. Descanse um pouco. Doctor / Medico
Drink water. Beba agua. Doctor / Medico
Get well soon! Melhoras! Doctor / Medico

Examples / Exemplos

Doctor asks / Medico pergunta

What's wrong?

O que esta errado? / Qual e o problema?

Patient answers / Paciente responde

I feel sick. My stomach hurts.

Eu me sinto mal. Meu estomago doi.

Patient answers / Paciente responde

I have a sore throat and a cough.

Eu tenho dor de garganta e tosse.

Doctor gives advice / Medico da conselho

Take this medicine and get some rest.

Tome este remedio e descanse um pouco.

Teacher Note:

Suggested time: 20 minutes

Role-play activity: Divide students into pairs. One student is the doctor and the other is the patient. The patient chooses a health problem from Unit 8.2. The doctor asks "What's wrong?" and the patient answers. Then the doctor gives advice. Switch roles after each conversation.

Scaffolding: Write the dialogue structure on the board so students can follow it step by step: (1) Greeting, (2) "What's wrong?", (3) "I have a ___ / My ___ hurts", (4) "Take this medicine. Get some rest.", (5) "Goodbye."

L1 support: For students who struggle, let them first practise the dialogue in Portuguese, then switch to English. The goal is to build confidence.

Unit 8.4

Reading & Writing / Leitura e Escrita

Reading / Leitura: Pedro is Sick / Pedro Esta Doente

Pedro is 14 years old. He is from Recife.

Today Pedro is sick. He is not at school.

He has a headache and a fever.

His mother takes him to the doctor.

The doctor says: "Take this medicine and drink water."

The doctor also says: "Get some rest. Stay home today."

Pedro goes home and rests in his bed.

Tomorrow he will feel better.

💡
Glossary / Glossario:
  • today = hoje
  • sick = doente
  • takes him = leva ele
  • says = diz
  • drink water = beba agua
  • stay home = fique em casa
  • goes home = vai para casa
  • rests = descansa
  • tomorrow = amanha
  • feel better = se sentir melhor
Exercise 7 True or False / Verdadeiro ou Falso

Read the text about Pedro. Write T (True / Verdadeiro) or F (False / Falso). / Leia o texto sobre Pedro. Escreva T (Verdadeiro) ou F (Falso).

1. Pedro is 14 years old. (Pedro tem 14 anos.) T
2. Pedro is at school today. (Pedro esta na escola hoje.) F
3. Pedro has a stomachache. (Pedro tem dor de barriga.) F
4. His mother takes him to the doctor. (A mae dele o leva ao medico.) T
5. The doctor says: "Go to school." (O medico diz: "Va a escola.") F
Exercise 8 Write About Your Health / Escreva Sobre Sua Saude

Write about a time you were sick OR describe how you feel today. Use the sentence starters below. / Escreva sobre uma vez que voce ficou doente OU descreva como voce se sente hoje. Use os inicios de frase abaixo.

Choose one / Escolha uma opcao:

Option A: A time I was sick / Uma vez que eu fiquei doente

Option B: How I feel today / Como eu me sinto hoje

1. My name is . (Meu nome e ___.) (student's name)
2. Today I feel . (Hoje eu me sinto ___.) (sick / healthy / fine)
3. I have a . (Eu tenho ___.) OR I am healthy. (Eu estou saudavel.) (headache / cold / stomachache / nothing -- or "I am healthy")
4. My hurts. (Meu/Minha ___ doi.) OR Nothing hurts. (Nada doi.) (head / stomach / back -- or "Nothing hurts")
5. I need to . (Eu preciso ___.) OR I am fine. (Eu estou bem.) (rest / see a doctor / drink water -- or "I am fine")
💡
Writing Tip / Dica de Escrita:

Remember: / Lembre-se:

  • Start every sentence with a capital letter. / Comece cada frase com letra maiuscula.
  • End every sentence with a period (.). / Termine cada frase com ponto (.).
  • "I" is always capital in English. / "I" (eu) e sempre maiusculo em ingles.
  • Use "a" before health problems: "I have a headache." / Use "a" antes de problemas de saude: "I have a headache."
Teacher Note:

Suggested time: 20 minutes

Reading activity: Read the text about Pedro aloud first. Then read it again, pausing at each sentence for students to repeat. Use the glossary to help students understand new words. Finally, ask students to read silently and answer the True/False questions.

Writing activity: Walk around the room and help students individually as they write Exercise 8. Encourage them to look back at the vocabulary tables and the reading passage for help. If a student is healthy today, encourage them to write "I am healthy" and "I feel fine" for practice.

Extension: If time allows, have 2-3 volunteers read their paragraphs aloud to the class. Ask the class: "Is [student's name] sick or healthy?"
