Teacher Note:

Suggested time: 25 minutes

Warm-up: Bring pictures of common foods (or draw them on the board). Point to each food and say the word in English. Have students repeat after you. Then ask: "Do you like ___?" and model the answer "Yes, I do" or "No, I don't."

Tip: Brazilian students will immediately recognize banana, chocolate, and pizza. Use these cognates to build confidence before introducing less familiar words like lettuce, grape, and watermelon.

L1 support: At B0 level, it is perfectly acceptable to use Portuguese to clarify meaning. The goal is comprehension first, production later.

📐 How to Describe Food (Como descrever alimentos)

To describe the flavor of a food, use this simple pattern (Para descrever o sabor de um alimento, use este padrao simples):

Pattern (Padrao) Example (Exemplo) Portugues
The ___ is ___. The cake is sweet. O bolo e doce.
It is ___. It is delicious! E delicioso!
___ is ___. Coffee is bitter. Cafe e amargo.

Note (Nota): In English, we use the verb "is" to connect the food to the flavor. In Portuguese, we also use "e" (ser) or "esta" (estar).

Teacher Note:

Activity: If possible, bring small food samples (a piece of lemon, sugar, salt, chocolate) and let students taste them. After each taste, ask: "Is it sweet? Is it sour?" Students answer with "Yes, it is" or "No, it isn't." This multisensory approach is very effective for beginners.

Alternative: Show pictures of foods and ask students to describe the flavor. Model the sentence pattern: "The ___ is ___."

Unit 3.3

Verb To Be — Part 1 (Verbo To Be — Parte 1)

📐 Verb To Be: Affirmative (Afirmativo)

The verb to be is one of the most important verbs in English. (O verbo to be e um dos verbos mais importantes do ingles.)

In Portuguese, to be = ser AND estar. In English, there is only ONE verb for both! (Em portugues, to be = ser E estar. Em ingles, so existe UM verbo para os dois!)

English Portugues Short Form (Forma curta)
I am Eu sou / Eu estou I'm
You are Voce e / Voce esta You're
He is Ele e / Ele esta He's
She is Ela e / Ela esta She's
It is Isso e / Isso esta It's

When do we use "to be"? (Quando usamos "to be"?)

  • Identity (Identidade): I am Carlos. (Eu sou Carlos.)
  • Feelings (Sentimentos): She is happy. (Ela esta feliz.)
  • Description (Descricao): It is big. (Isso e grande.)
  • Food (Alimentos): The cake is sweet. (O bolo e doce.)

Common Adjectives with "To Be" (Adjetivos comuns com "To Be")

English Portugues Example Sentence
hungry com fome I am hungry. (Eu estou com fome.)
thirsty com sede He is thirsty. (Ele esta com sede.)

sad triste He is sad. (Ele esta triste.)

small pequeno(a) The grape is small. (A uva e pequena.)
good bom / boa The food is good. (A comida e boa.)
bad ruim / mau The milk is bad. (O leite esta ruim.)

📌
Remember (Lembre-se):

In Portuguese, we say "Eu tenho fome" (I have hunger). But in English, we say "I am hungry" (NOT "I have hungry"!).

Em portugues, dizemos "Eu tenho fome". Mas em ingles, dizemos "I am hungry" (NAO "I have hungry"!).

The same applies for: (O mesmo vale para:)

  • I am thirsty. (Eu estou com sede.) NOT "I have thirsty."
  • I am cold. (Eu estou com frio.) NOT "I have cold."
  • I am hot. (Eu estou com calor.) NOT "I have hot."

Dialogue: At the Cafe (Dialogo: No cafe)

At the school cafeteria — Na cantina da escola
Lucas: Hi, Ana! How are you?
Ana: Hi, Lucas! I am hungry! And you?
Lucas: I am hungry too! And I am thirsty.
Ana: Do you like rice and beans?
Lucas: Yes, I do! Rice and beans is delicious.
Ana: And the chicken is good today. It is hot and fresh.
Lucas: I like chicken! And do you like juice?
Ana: Yes! I like orange juice. It is sweet and cold. Delicious!
Lucas: I am happy. The food is good today!
Translation of the dialogue (Traducao do dialogo)

Lucas: Oi, Ana! Como voce esta?

Ana: Oi, Lucas! Eu estou com fome! E voce?

Lucas: Eu tambem estou com fome! E estou com sede.

Ana: Voce gosta de arroz e feijao?

Lucas: Sim! Arroz e feijao e delicioso.

Ana: E o frango esta bom hoje. Esta quente e fresco.

Lucas: Eu gosto de frango! E voce gosta de suco?

Ana: Sim! Eu gosto de suco de laranja. E doce e gelado. Delicioso!

Lucas: Eu estou feliz. A comida esta boa hoje!

Teacher Note:

Suggested time: 30 minutes

L1 interference: The biggest challenge here is that Portuguese uses "ter" (to have) for physical states like hunger and thirst, while English uses "to be". Write on the board: "Eu TENHO fome = I AM hungry" and drill this pattern several times.

Dialogue activity: Have students read the dialogue in pairs. Then have them change the foods and flavors to create their own version. For example: "I like fish. Fish is delicious."

Simplification: At B0 level, focus ONLY on the affirmative forms (I am, you are, he/she/it is). Negative and question forms will come in a later chapter.

Reading & Writing (Leitura e Escrita)

Ana's Breakfast (O cafe da manha da Ana) Read the text carefully, then answer the questions below. (Leia o texto com atencao e depois responda as perguntas abaixo.)

My name is Ana. I am from Belo Horizonte. Every morning, I eat breakfast at home.

I like bread with cheese. The bread is fresh and the cheese is delicious. I drink coffee with milk. The coffee is hot and sweet. My mom drinks coffee too, but she likes it bitter, without sugar.

I also eat a banana. The banana is sweet and good. My brother Lucas likes orange juice. The juice is cold and fresh. He is always thirsty in the morning!

I am happy when I eat a good breakfast. Breakfast is important!

Original text for Incluir English Workbook
💡
Vocabulary Help (Ajuda de vocabulario):
  • every morning = toda manha
  • breakfast = cafe da manha
  • at home = em casa
  • with = com
  • without = sem
  • sugar = acucar
  • also = tambem
  • always = sempre
  • when = quando
  • important = importante
Teacher Note:

Pre-reading activity: Ask students: "What do you eat for breakfast? What do you drink?" Accept answers in Portuguese, then help them say the food names in English.

Reading strategy: Have students underline all food words in one color and all forms of the verb to be in another color while reading.

Post-reading: Read the text aloud slowly. Then read it again and have students repeat each sentence after you. This builds pronunciation confidence at B0 level.
