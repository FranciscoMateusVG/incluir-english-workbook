Exercise 1 Simple Past or Past Perfect?

Complete each sentence with the correct form of the verb in parentheses: Simple Past or Past Perfect (had + past participle). Think about which action happened first.

1. When we (arrive) at the bus stop, the bus (already / leave). arrived ... had already left
2. She (be) nervous because she (never / speak) in public before. was ... had never spoken
3. After the guests (eat) dinner, they (watch) a film. had eaten ... watched
4. By the time the manager (call), I (finish) the report. called ... had finished
5. He (not / can) get in because he (forget) his keys. couldn't ... had forgotten
6. I (recognize) the song because I (hear) it before. recognized ... had heard
7. She first (open) the windows, and then she (clean) the room. opened ... cleaned
8. They (not / meet) before the conference started last Monday. hadn't met

Exercise 3 Find and Correct the Mistake

Each sentence has ONE mistake with the Past Perfect or Simple Past. Rewrite the sentence correctly on the line.

1.

When I got to the office, my colleagues have already gone home.

Correct: When I got to the office, my colleagues had already gone home.
2.

She was tired because she didn't sleep well the night before.

Correct: She was tired because she hadn't slept well the night before.
3.

After we had paid the bill, we had left the restaurant.

Correct: After we had paid the bill, we left the restaurant.
4.

By the time the doctor arrived, the patient had feel better.

Correct: By the time the doctor arrived, the patient had felt better. (or: the patient felt better)
5.

They had never saw snow before they traveled to Canada.

Correct: They had never seen snow before they traveled to Canada.
6.

He didn't recognize the town because it changed so much.

Correct: He didn't recognize the town because it had changed so much.
Exercise 4 Combine the Sentences

Combine each pair of sentences into ONE sentence using the word in parentheses. Put the earlier event in the Past Perfect and the later event in the Simple Past.

1.

The meeting started. I arrived at the office. (by the time)

By the time I arrived at the office, the meeting had already started.
2.

She locked all the doors. She went to bed. (after)

After she had locked all the doors, she went to bed.
3.

He saved enough money. He booked the holiday. (before)

He had saved enough money before he booked the holiday.
4.

The shop closed. We got there. (when)

When we got there, the shop had already closed.
5.

I never tried sushi. I visited Japan last year. (before)

I had never tried sushi before I visited Japan last year.
6.

The film already began. We sat down. (when)

When we sat down, the film had already begun.
