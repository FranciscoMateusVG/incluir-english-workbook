Exercise 2 Join the Sentences

Combine each pair of sentences into one sentence using a relative pronoun (who, which, that, where, when, or whose). Keep the first sentence as the main idea.

1.

The man is a doctor. He lives next door.

Correct: The man who lives next door is a doctor.
2.

I lost a phone. The phone was brand new.

Correct: The phone which/that I lost was brand new.
3.

This is the bakery. The bakery opens at six in the morning.

Correct: This is the bakery which/that opens at six in the morning.
4.

That is the square. Old men play dominoes there.

Correct: That is the square where old men play dominoes.
5.

The girl won the prize. Her project was the best.

Correct: The girl whose project was the best won the prize.
6.

Salvador is a beautiful city. My grandmother lives there.

Correct: Salvador, where my grandmother lives, is a beautiful city. (non-defining — Salvador is already named)

  • c) The Amazon River, that flows through Brazil, is very long.
4.
  • a) The boy who's bike is broken needs help.
  • b) The boy whom bike is broken needs help.
  • c) The boy whose bike is broken needs help. ✓ correct
5.
  • a) The house where I was born is for sale. ✓ correct
  • b) The house which I was born is for sale.
  • c) The house when I was born is for sale.
