# AI Study Missions

## Purpose

Students need practice outside class, especially speaking and listening practice. Many students have a cellphone, and ChatGPT Voice can become an extra practice partner when a teacher is not available.

AI should not replace the teacher or the class. It should extend practice between classes.

The model is:

```text
Class -> AI mission at home -> review in next class
```

## Basic principle

Do not tell students, "Use AI to study English."

Give them a specific mission.

Each AI mission should tell students:

- What to open.
- What to say.
- What the goal is.
- Which phrases to practice.
- What to bring back to the next class.

## Access assumptions

As of July 18, 2026, ChatGPT has a free tier, and ChatGPT Voice has limited free access. Exact limits and availability can change depending on plan, region, app version, and OpenAI's current usage policies.

For students, the practical instruction is:

1. Install the ChatGPT app.
2. Create or log into a free account.
3. Open Voice mode.
4. Allow microphone access.
5. Read the mission prompt aloud.

Teacher note:

Because free limits can change, AI practice should be an accelerator, not a required condition for passing the course.

## Three mission types

### 1. Conversation Trainer

Use this when the goal is to answer questions and speak more naturally.

Example prompt:

```text
Act as my English teacher. I am a beginner English student from Brazil.
Practice English conversation with me.
Ask me one question at a time.
Speak slowly.
Use easy English.
If I make a mistake, correct me briefly in Portuguese and ask me to repeat.
```

Best for:

- Simple Present routines.
- Likes and dislikes.
- Family.
- Work and school.
- Weekend activities.
- Personal information.

### 2. Roleplay Partner

Use this when the goal is survival English.

Example prompt:

```text
Act as a hotel receptionist.
I am a beginner English student from Brazil.
Practice a hotel check-in roleplay with me.
Ask me questions one at a time.
Speak slowly.
If I do not understand, rephrase in easier English.
After the roleplay, correct my main mistakes in Portuguese.
```

Best for:

- Airport.
- Hotel.
- Restaurant.
- Pharmacy.
- Directions.
- Customer service.
- Lost item report.

### 3. Correction Coach

Use this when the goal is to improve written sentences or prepare speaking.

Example prompt:

```text
Correct my English sentences.
Explain the corrections in Portuguese like I am a beginner.
Do not change everything. Keep my ideas simple.
After each correction, give me one more example.

My sentences:
1. ________________________________________________
2. ________________________________________________
3. ________________________________________________
```

Best for:

- Homework sentences.
- Paragraphs.
- Preparing a short presentation.
- Correcting common grammar mistakes.

## Example: Simple Present AI mission

```text
AI Mission: Talk about your routine

Time:
10 minutes

Mode:
ChatGPT Voice

Prompt:
"Act as my English teacher. I am a beginner English student from Brazil.
Practice Simple Present with me.
Ask me questions about my daily routine, one question at a time.
Speak slowly.
If I make a mistake, correct me in Portuguese and ask me to repeat.
Use easy English."

Goal:
Answer at least 5 questions.

Useful phrases:
- I wake up at ___.
- I work in the morning.
- I study English on Saturdays.
- I don't work on Sundays.
- I usually ___.

Bring to next class:
- One sentence you said well.
- One mistake ChatGPT corrected.
- One new word you learned.
```

Next class review:

```text
Tell your partner:
What questions did ChatGPT ask you?
What mistake did it correct?
Say three sentences about your routine.
```

## Example: B4 airport problem mission

```text
AI Mission: Report a lost suitcase

Time:
10 minutes

Mode:
ChatGPT Voice

Prompt:
"Act as an airport worker.
I am a beginner English student from Brazil.
My suitcase did not arrive.
Practice a roleplay with me in English.
Ask me questions one at a time.
Speak slowly.
If I make a grammar mistake, correct me briefly in Portuguese.
Help me use these phrases:
My bag was lost.
It was checked in at Sao Paulo.
I was told to come here.
What should I do now?"

Goal:
Complete the roleplay.

Useful phrases:
- My bag was lost.
- It was checked in at Sao Paulo.
- I was told to come here.
- What should I do now?
- Could you repeat that?

Bring to next class:
- One phrase you used.
- One question ChatGPT asked.
- One mistake ChatGPT corrected.
```

## Example: Pharmacy mission

```text
AI Mission: Explain a health problem

Time:
10 minutes

Mode:
ChatGPT Voice

Prompt:
"Act as a pharmacist.
I am a beginner English student from Brazil.
Practice a pharmacy conversation with me.
Ask about my symptoms.
Speak slowly and use easy English.
If I make a mistake, correct me briefly in Portuguese.
Help me ask for medicine and understand basic advice."

Goal:
Explain at least 2 symptoms and ask for help.

Useful phrases:
- I have a headache.
- I have a sore throat.
- Do I need a prescription?
- How often should I take it?
- Could you speak more slowly?

Bring to next class:
- One symptom you practiced.
- One question you asked.
- One new word you learned.
```

## Safety and quality guidance

Students should know:

- ChatGPT can make mistakes.
- ChatGPT is for practice, not official translation or legal/medical advice.
- Do not share private information, passwords, documents, addresses, or sensitive personal data.
- For health, legal, immigration, or emergency situations, talk to a real professional.
- If ChatGPT says something confusing, ask the teacher next class.

Teacher note:

For roleplays involving health, immigration, or money, keep the mission focused on language practice, not real-life decision-making.

## Accessibility and inclusion

Not every student will have a phone, internet, quiet space, or enough free ChatGPT access.

AI practice must always have an offline alternative.

Offline alternatives:

- Practice the same dialogue with a classmate.
- Read the useful phrases out loud five times.
- Write five sentences using the formula.
- Record audio on the phone without sending it.
- Teach three phrases to someone at home.
- Prepare answers for next class on paper.

Example offline version:

```text
No phone or no internet?

Practice these phrases out loud five times:
- I wake up at ___.
- I work/study ___.
- I don't work on Sundays.
- What time do you wake up?
- Do you study English every day?

Then write five sentences about your routine.
```

## Accountability without pressure

The teacher should use the AI mission at the beginning of the next class.

Simple review options:

- Ask students to share one sentence they practiced.
- Ask students to share one corrected mistake.
- Ask students to teach one new word to a partner.
- Ask students to repeat the roleplay with a classmate.

The goal is not to punish students who did not do it. The goal is to make extra practice feel useful and connected to class.

## Implementation idea

Each lesson should end with:

1. One AI mission card.
2. One offline alternative.
3. One next-class review question.

This keeps the system simple for volunteers and useful for students.
