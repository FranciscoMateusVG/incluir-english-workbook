# Lesson Prototype: Simple Present

## Class mission

Students can describe their daily routine and ask about another person's routine.

Simple Present should not be taught only as "add `s` to he/she/it." It should be taught as the structure students use to talk about daily life: routines, habits, facts, work, school, likes, and repeated actions.

## Course model

This lesson follows the Incluir class model:

1. Formula
2. Practice
3. Real Use

Approximate timing for a two-hour class:

| Time | Stage | Purpose |
|---:|---|---|
| 0-35 min | Formula | Teach the pattern and show why it matters. |
| 35-80 min | Practice | Students repeat, complete, correct, and build sentences. |
| 80-120 min | Real Use | Students interview a partner and report the partner's routine. |

## Stage 1: Formula

### Main use

Simple Present is used for:

- Routines.
- Habits.
- Facts.
- Likes and dislikes.
- Work and school life.
- Repeated actions.

### Core formula

Affirmative:

```text
I / You / We / They + verb
He / She / It + verb + s/es
```

Examples:

```text
I work.
You study English.
They live near here.
She works.
He studies English.
```

Negative:

```text
I / You / We / They + don't + verb
He / She / It + doesn't + verb
```

Examples:

```text
I don't work on Sundays.
They don't study at night.
She doesn't work on Sundays.
He doesn't live near here.
```

Questions:

```text
Do + I / you / we / they + verb?
Does + he / she / it + verb?
```

Examples:

```text
Do you work?
Do they study English?
Does she work?
Does he live near here?
```

Information questions:

```text
What time do you wake up?
Where do you work?
When do you study English?
What do you do on Sundays?
```

### Key teaching point

After `does` or `doesn't`, the main verb returns to the base form.

Correct:

```text
She works.
She doesn't work.
Does she work?
```

Common mistakes:

```text
She work.
She doesn't works.
Does she works?
```

### Useful time expressions

```text
every day
in the morning
in the afternoon
at night
on Mondays
on weekends
usually
sometimes
never
```

### Board plan

```text
MISSION: Talk about routines

I work.
She works.

I don't work.
She doesn't work.

Do you work?
Does she work?

What time do you wake up?
Where do you work/study?
What do you do on Sundays?
```

## Stage 2: Practice

The goal of practice is repetition with correction. Students should see, hear, and say the target structure many times before the final speaking task.

### Practice A: Complete the sentences

Fill in the blank with the correct Simple Present form.

```text
1. I ______ at 7 every morning. (wake up)
2. She ______ coffee before work. (drink)
3. They ______ English on Saturdays. (study)
4. He ______ at night. (work)
5. We ______ near the school. (live)
6. My brother ______ soccer on weekends. (play)
```

Answers:

```text
1. wake up
2. drinks
3. study
4. works
5. live
6. plays
```

### Practice B: Make the sentences negative

```text
1. I work on Sundays.
2. She studies at night.
3. They live near here.
4. He drinks coffee.
5. We watch TV in the morning.
```

Answers:

```text
1. I don't work on Sundays.
2. She doesn't study at night.
3. They don't live near here.
4. He doesn't drink coffee.
5. We don't watch TV in the morning.
```

### Practice C: Correct the mistakes

```text
1. She work at a store.
2. He don't study English.
3. Do she live near here?
4. I studies at night.
5. Does he works on Saturdays?
```

Answers:

```text
1. She works at a store.
2. He doesn't study English.
3. Does she live near here?
4. I study at night.
5. Does he work on Saturdays?
```

### Practice D: Build questions

Use the words to make questions.

```text
1. you / work / on Saturdays?
2. what time / she / wake up?
3. where / they / study?
4. he / drink / coffee?
5. what / you / do / on Sundays?
```

Answers:

```text
1. Do you work on Saturdays?
2. What time does she wake up?
3. Where do they study?
4. Does he drink coffee?
5. What do you do on Sundays?
```

### Pronunciation focus

Practice the third-person `s` naturally, without overloading students with theory.

Useful repetition:

```text
She works.
He studies.
She lives near here.
He watches TV.
```

Teacher note:

Do not spend too long explaining all pronunciation rules for final `s`. Model, repeat, correct gently, and move on.

## Stage 3: Real Use

### Final task: Routine interview

Students work in pairs.

Student A asks Student B:

```text
What time do you wake up?
Do you work or study?
Where do you work?
When do you study English?
Do you study English every day?
What do you do on Sundays?
Do you drink coffee?
Do you watch TV at night?
```

Then Student A reports Student B's routine:

```text
Maria wakes up at 6.
She works at a supermarket.
She studies English on Saturdays.
She doesn't work on Sundays.
She drinks coffee in the morning.
She watches TV at night.
```

Then students switch roles.

### Why this task works

The interview makes students practice questions with `do`.

The report makes students practice third-person `s`.

The task is personal, practical, and reusable in real conversation.

## Exit check

At the end of class, the teacher checks:

Can the student say four sentences about their own routine?

Example:

```text
I wake up at 7.
I work in the morning.
I study English on Saturdays.
I don't work on Sundays.
```

Can the student say four sentences about a partner's routine?

Example:

```text
Ana wakes up at 6.
She works in a store.
She studies English on Saturdays.
She doesn't work on Sundays.
```

If students can do both without reading every word from the page, the class worked.

## Optional homework

Students write a short paragraph about their routine.

```text
My routine

I wake up at ______.
I work/study ______.
I study English ______.
I usually ______.
I don't ______.
```

For extra practice, students also write about one family member:

```text
My mother/father/sister/brother ______.
He/She ______.
He/She doesn't ______.
```

## Volunteer reminder

The goal is not to explain every detail of Simple Present in one class.

The goal is for students to use Simple Present to talk about routines.

Formula first. Practice second. Real use at the end.
